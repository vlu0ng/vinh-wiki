# Bubble Finance

Bubble finance is [[david-stockman|David Stockman]]'s term for a monetary regime in which central banks repeatedly prop up asset prices, suppress interest rates, and encourage speculation rather than allowing markets to clear. In his account, bubble finance is the financial mechanism behind modern [[crony-capitalism]].

## Key Properties

- **Asset-price reflation** — downturns are met by policies that revive stocks, housing, credit, or leveraged buyout markets.
- **Distorted risk pricing** — cheap money makes bad debts and speculative bets look safer than they are.
- **Bailout expectations** — investors assume the state or central bank will intervene when losses threaten large financial institutions.
- **Real-economy weakness** — financial gains mask stagnant productivity, wages, and productive investment.
