# Focusing Question

Gary Keller's core tool from *The ONE Thing*:

> **"What's the ONE thing I can do such that by doing it, everything else will be easier or unnecessary?"**

## How It Works

The question forces prioritization by *elimination* — not adding the most important task to a list, but identifying
the single action whose completion would most change the landscape of what comes next. The key phrase: "easier or
unnecessary." A good ONE Thing doesn't just accomplish something; it clears the path for future progress.

## Scales of Application

The question applies at every time horizon:
- **Life**: What is the ONE thing I want my life to be about?
- **5-year**: What is the ONE thing I can do in the next five years toward that life?
- **Annual**: What ONE thing would make this year the foundation for the next?
- **Monthly/weekly**: What ONE thing can I do this week such that everything else becomes easier or unnecessary?
- **Daily**: What is the first, most important thing to accomplish today?

Used consistently, this creates the [[the-one-thing|domino effect]] — each completion sequences into the next priority.

## Why It Works

- Forces *sequential* thinking over *simultaneous* prioritization
- Surfaces the *lead domino* — the action that, by moving, moves other things
- Eliminates the decision overhead of managing complex priority systems
- Creates momentum through completion rather than perpetual work-in-progress

## Relationship to Other Frameworks

- [[inversion]]: the focusing question is a form of forward-reasoning inversion — start with the outcome you want,
  work backward to the most leveraged action
- [[mental-models]]: a heuristic that belongs in the working toolbox alongside Munger's checklist
- [[slow-productivity]]: Newport's "do fewer things" is the structural twin — reduce active obligations (Newport)
  and within those obligations, identify the most leveraged action (Keller)

## Limitation

The question assumes you can identify what "easier or unnecessary" means — which requires a clear vision of what
you're optimizing for. Without that, the focusing question just gives a feeling of clarity without the substance.
