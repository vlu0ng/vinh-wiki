# General Purpose Technology

A general purpose technology is a technology that spreads across many sectors, enables complementary innovations, and reshapes how work is organized. Ethan Mollick frames [[large-language-models]] as a general purpose technology because they can affect nearly every form of cognitive work rather than a single narrow industry.

## Key Properties

- **Broad applicability** — useful across many domains rather than one specialized workflow.
- **Complementary innovation** — value grows as people redesign processes, institutions, and habits around it.
- **Productivity effects** — the technology changes the cost, speed, or quality of many tasks.
- **Uneven diffusion** — gains depend on experimentation, adoption, and the ability to identify where the technology works.

## AI Context

In [[living-and-working-with-ai]], the general-purpose character of LLMs explains why their impact is hard to forecast from a list of features. Their value depends on how people map the [[jagged-frontier]] of capability in their own work.
