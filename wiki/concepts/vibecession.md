# Vibecession

A term coined by Kyla Scanlon in 2022 to describe an economic situation where:
- **Hard indicators** (GDP, unemployment, corporate earnings) are technically healthy, or at least not recessionary
- **Soft indicators** (consumer sentiment, public mood, social media tone) suggest people feel like they're in a
  recession

The "vibe" is the gap between what the economy looks like on a spreadsheet and what it feels like to live in it.

## Why It Matters

The vibecession concept reveals several important truths about how economies actually work:

1. **Expectations are economic forces**: Depressed consumer sentiment can reduce spending, which *causes* the
   slowdown people feared. Vibes can become self-fulfilling.

2. **Official metrics are lagging and partial**: CPI, unemployment rate, and GDP don't capture housing affordability,
   debt burden, income inequality, or the psychological toll of economic instability.

3. **Media amplification**: Engagement-optimized economic coverage rewards alarmism, inflating vibes in the negative
   direction.

## The 2022 Context

Scanlon coined the term in 2022 when: inflation was high but employment was strong; real wages for many workers had
recovered; yet consumer sentiment was near historic lows. Conventional economics couldn't fully explain the gap.
Behavioral economics and media analysis could.

## Broader Implication

The vibecession is a case study in why economic literacy requires both technical knowledge (what does the CPI measure?)
and human insight (how do people actually experience and respond to economic conditions?). Pure data without behavioral
context is incomplete.
