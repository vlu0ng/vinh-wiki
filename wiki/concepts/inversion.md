# Inversion

Inversion is a problem-solving mental model: instead of asking only how to succeed, ask what would guarantee failure, then avoid or remove those causes. [[charlie-munger|Charlie Munger]] popularized the method through the maxim "invert, always invert," attributed to mathematician Carl Jacobi.

## Key Properties

- **Failure-first reasoning** — identify the obvious ways a system, decision, or plan could break.
- **Avoidance as strategy** — many problems are easier to prevent than to solve after the fact.
- **Bias reduction** — inversion counters wishful thinking by forcing attention onto risks, constraints, and incentives.

## Examples

- To improve learning, ask: "What would make me stop practicing?" then design against friction, boredom, and unclear feedback.
- To build a good organization, ask: "What incentives would create politics, blame, or short-termism?" then remove them.
- To make better investments, ask: "What could permanently impair this business?" before asking how high the upside might be.
