# AI in Education

## Overview / Core Thesis

Salman Khan's *Brave New Words* argues that AI — specifically large language models like GPT-4 — is not a cheating
tool to be banned but the first technology capable of fulfilling the long-held promise of personalized, one-on-one
tutoring for every student on the planet. The title signals a call to rewrite education's operating assumptions rather
than protect them.

## Khanmigo: The AI Tutor Model

Khan Academy launched Khanmigo in March 2023 as one of the first educational deployments of GPT-4. Key design choices:

- **Socratic, not answer-giving**: The system was explicitly prompted to ask leading questions rather than provide
  answers. GPT-4's "steerability" (ability to maintain a role across a conversation) was essential — GPT-3.5 would
  revert to giving answers despite instructions.
- **Safe by design**: Extensive edge-case prompting before launch; distinct tone/voice for the platform; guardrails
  against inappropriate conversations with minors.
- **Beyond tutoring**: Khanmigo also role-plays historical and literary figures, acts as a debate partner, serves as
  a guidance counselor and career coach, and supports teachers with lesson planning and student progress analysis.

## The Bloomian Vision

Benjamin Bloom's 1984 "2 Sigma Problem" showed that one-on-one tutored students performed 2 standard deviations above
classroom-taught students. The problem: human tutoring is too expensive to scale. AI tutoring is Khan's proposed
solution — not a replacement for teachers but a force multiplier that finally makes the 2-sigma outcome accessible.

## Concerns and Tensions

- **Writing and cognition**: AI that writes *for* students may atrophy the thinking that writing develops. Khan argues
  that good AI writing tools can be used *alongside* students to develop their ideas rather than replace the process.
  See [[cognitive-atrophy-and-ai]].
- **Equity**: AI tutoring could widen gaps if high-income families use it to accelerate while lower-income students
  fall behind, or it could close gaps if deployed intentionally to underserved schools.
- **Assessment**: If AI can write essays, traditional essay assessments lose validity. Khan advocates for AI-proctored
  oral exams and projects as replacements.

## Employment and the Future of Work

Later chapters address AI's impact on the labor market. Khan's stance: history shows that technology displaces tasks,
not workers — but transitions are painful and require active investment in education and retraining.

## Open Questions

- **Can AI tutoring replicate the motivational and relational components of great human teaching?**  
  AI can emulate relational scaffolding (patience, personalized framing) and lower social pressure for low-stakes failure, supporting a [[growth-mindset]]. However, genuine motivation relies on social-emotional bonds and reciprocal empathy — transactions unique to human teachers. The developmental tension and hybrid pedagogical structures are synthesized in [[ai-tutoring-and-human-motivation]].
- How do we prevent AI tutoring from becoming another vector for [[jagged-frontier]] failures — excellent at some
  tasks, dangerously wrong at others the student can't detect?
