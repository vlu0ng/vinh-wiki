# Glucose Revolution

## Overview / Core Thesis

Jessie Inchauspé (*Glucose Goddess*) argues that glucose management is the highest-leverage single intervention for
improving metabolic health. Flattening glucose curves — reducing the amplitude of spikes after meals — improves
energy, mood, weight, hormonal health, and long-term disease risk without requiring extreme dietary restriction.

The cockpit metaphor: we are both passengers and pilots of our own bodies. Glucose is the lever with the biggest
bang for its buck because it is the only metabolic metric most people can monitor continuously (via CGM), and
because it is downstream of so much else — fructose and insulin curves follow glucose curves.

## Why Glucose Matters

Only 12% of Americans are metabolically healthy (per 2019 research). Glucose dysregulation is implicated in:
- Weight gain and difficulty losing weight
- Energy crashes and chronic fatigue
- Mood instability, anxiety, brain fog
- Hormonal disruption (PCOS, missed periods)
- Type 2 diabetes, insulin resistance
- Cardiovascular disease
- Skin conditions (acne, inflammation)

## The Glucose Curve Framework

Inchauspé uses CGM data to visualize how different meals and behaviors affect blood glucose over time. The goal is not
minimizing glucose — we need it — but flattening the curves to avoid large spikes and subsequent crashes.

### The Biology (Simplified)
- Plants make glucose via photosynthesis (the Calvin-Benson-Bassham cycle)
- Animals store glucose as glycogen in liver/muscle; excess becomes fat
- The pancreas releases insulin in response to glucose; chronic high insulin → insulin resistance → type 2 diabetes
- Fructose travels a different metabolic pathway but is co-packaged with glucose in food; flattening glucose curves
  also flattens fructose and insulin curves

## The 10 Glucose Hacks

Inchauspé's key interventions (not a complete diet, but targeted tactics):
1. **Eat foods in the right order**: fiber → protein/fat → starches/sugars
2. **Add a veggie starter** to meals
3. **Stop counting calories, start counting glucose impact**
4. **Flatten your breakfast curve** (savory > sweet)
5. **Have any sweet things as dessert**, not on an empty stomach
6. **Vinegar before meals** (dampens glucose spike)
7. **Movement after eating** (muscles absorb glucose without insulin)
8. **If you snack, go savory**
9. **Don't drink your glucose** (juice, soda, even "healthy" smoothies spike fast)
10. **A spoonful of fat or protein before meals with high sugar**

## Important Caveats

Inchauspé explicitly warns: glucose isn't everything. Industrial processed oils, alcohol, and other factors matter
for health but don't show up as glucose spikes. And context is everything — whether a food is "good" or "bad" depends
on what you would eat instead.
