# Kyla Scanlon

Economic commentator, content creator, and author known for making macroeconomics accessible and human. Former asset
management analyst at Capital Group; pivoted to full-time content creation and writing after her short-form economic
videos gained a large following.

## Background

Majored in finance, economics, and data analytics in college. Began writing about markets as a teenager (blog:
*Scanlon on Stocks*). Her gift is translating technical economic concepts into relatable language while taking the
human/behavioral dimension seriously — a combination that most economics education and commentary lacks.

## Key Contributions

- Coined **"vibecession"** (2022) — the phenomenon where economic indicators are technically healthy but consumer
  sentiment is depressed; feelings can become self-fulfilling. The term captured a genuine gap in economic vocabulary
  and spread widely.
- *In This Economy?* (2024) — a first-principles guide to economic literacy; argues that the economy is fundamentally
  "people peopling around" and must be understood through both mechanics and behavioral psychology
- Pioneered accessible economic education on short-form video, demonstrating that economic literacy is a media/
  communication problem as much as a pedagogical one
