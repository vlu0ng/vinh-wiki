# Sal Khan

Founder and CEO of Khan Academy, the nonprofit that provides free, world-class education to anyone anywhere. Former
hedge fund analyst who began tutoring his cousins remotely via Yahoo Doodle; the sessions became YouTube videos;
the videos became Khan Academy.

## Background

Holds degrees from MIT (mathematics, computer science) and Harvard Business School. His educational philosophy
is grounded in **mastery learning**: students should demonstrate mastery of a concept before moving on, rather
than being advanced with their cohort on a time-based schedule. This is the opposite of how most schools work.

## Key Contributions

- Built Khan Academy into the world's largest free educational platform (~150M registered users)
- Identified and documented Benjamin Bloom's "2 Sigma Problem" as the mission framing for AI tutoring: human
  one-on-one tutoring produces 2-standard-deviation gains, but human tutoring doesn't scale; AI might close that gap
- Launched Khanmigo (March 2023) — one of the first large-scale GPT-4 educational deployments, designed as a
  Socratic tutor rather than an answer machine
- Authored *Brave New Words* (2024) as a manifesto for embracing rather than banning AI in education

## Design Philosophy for AI in Education

- AI should *ask* questions, not *give* answers (Socratic method)
- Safety and pedagogical soundness must be designed in before launch, not retrofitted
- The goal is not automation of teaching but *augmentation* — freeing teachers from administrative burden while
  deepening student engagement
