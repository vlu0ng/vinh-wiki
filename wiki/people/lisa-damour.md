# Lisa Damour

Clinical psychologist, author, and leading expert on the psychological development of teenage girls.

## Background

Damour holds a Ph.D. in clinical psychology and maintains a private psychotherapy practice focused on adolescents and their families. She is an instructor in the Department of Psychological Sciences at Case Western Reserve University, and serves as a consulting psychologist and director of the Center for Research on Girls at Laurel School, an independent all-girls school in Ohio. She is also a mother of two daughters.

## Key Contributions

- **Untangled** (2016) — a developmental framework for understanding teenage girls through seven distinct "strands"; reframes adolescent behavior as purposeful development rather than chaos to be endured
- **Under Pressure** (2019) — examines the stress and anxiety epidemic among teenage girls; practical guidance for parents and educators
- Regular contributor to the New York Times and consultant to media on adolescent issues
- Adaptation of Anna Freud's developmental strands framework into a practical, parent-facing tool
