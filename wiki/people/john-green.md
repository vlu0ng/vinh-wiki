# John Green

American author and public intellectual known primarily for young adult fiction (*The Fault in Our Stars*, *Looking for Alaska*) who has become a significant voice in global health advocacy and science communication.

## Relevance to This Wiki

Author of *Everything Is Tuberculosis* (2025), a nonfiction investigation into why tuberculosis — curable since the 1950s — still kills over a million people per year.

## Key Arguments

- TB's persistence is a moral and political failure, not a scientific one
- How we **frame** illness determines who receives care and who dies — framing is never neutral
- The cure for TB exists; the problem is that *the cure is where the disease is not*
- Health crises are inseparable from [[health-equity]] — poverty, race, and geography determine access to medicine

## Personal Connection

Green's great-uncle **Stokes Goodrich** died of TB in 1930 in an Asheville sanatorium at age 29. This personal history anchors the book's emotional argument.

## Work with Partners in Health

Green and his wife Sarah visited Lakka Government Hospital in Sierra Leone — supported by **[[partners-in-health|Partners in Health]]** — where he encountered active TB cases, including a child patient named Henry, and multidrug-resistant TB strains.

## Media and Outreach

Green co-created the educational YouTube channel *Crash Course* and *Mental Floss*. His public platform has brought global health topics — especially TB and neglected tropical diseases — to mainstream audiences who would not otherwise encounter them.
