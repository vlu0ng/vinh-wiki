# Carol Dweck

Professor of Psychology at Stanford University; originator of **[[growth-mindset]]** theory.

## Key Work

*Mindset: The New Psychology of Success* (2006) — the foundational text on fixed vs. growth mindsets. Based on decades of research on motivation, achievement, and how people respond to failure.

## Core Contribution

Identified two implicit theories of intelligence that people hold:
- **Entity theory (fixed mindset)**: ability is a fixed trait — you have a certain amount and that's it
- **Incremental theory (growth mindset)**: ability develops through effort and learning

Children and adults who hold an incremental theory show greater persistence, take on harder challenges, recover better from setbacks, and ultimately achieve more.

## Influence on Mathematics Education

[[jo-boaler|Jo Boaler]] applied Dweck's framework specifically to mathematics, showing that math culture in schools is uniquely prone to fixed-mindset messaging. See [[mathematics-education]] for full synthesis.
