# Jo Boaler

Professor of Mathematics Education at Stanford University; founder of **YouCubed**, a research and outreach initiative that provides open mathematics tasks and professional development resources to teachers worldwide.

## Key Contributions

- Applied [[growth-mindset]] theory (developed by [[carol-dweck]]) specifically to mathematics learning
- Identified the specific ways traditional math instruction creates fixed-mindset students — especially through timed tests, ability grouping, and emphasis on speed
- Developed "open tasks" (low-floor, high-ceiling problems) that allow multiple entry points and solution paths
- Demonstrated via research that mixed-ability grouping with role-based structures closes achievement gaps

## Major Work

*Mathematical Mindsets: Unleashing Students' Potential through Creative Mathematics, Inspiring Messages and Innovative Teaching* (1st ed. 2016; 2nd ed. 2022, Jossey-Bass)

Full synthesis: [[mathematics-education]]

## YouCubed

Stanford-based research center and teacher support hub. Provides:
- Free open math tasks for K–12 teachers
- Professional development workshops
- Research summaries on brain science and math learning
- "Week of Inspirational Math" — annual curriculum used by millions of students

## Key Claims

1. Timed tests activate the amygdala and suppress mathematical reasoning ability — they measure anxiety, not competence
2. Mistakes made and noticed produce more synaptic growth than correct answers
3. "Speed = smart" is a cultural myth that disproportionately harms girls and students of color
4. Visual and spatial approaches to mathematics develop deeper understanding than symbol manipulation alone
