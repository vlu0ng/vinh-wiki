# Charlie Munger

Charles Thomas Munger (1924–2023) — vice chairman of Berkshire Hathaway, [[warren-buffett|Warren Buffett]]'s longtime partner, lawyer, and one of the most original thinkers in American business.

## Background

Born in Omaha, Nebraska, on January 1, 1924, Munger grew up during the Depression — an experience that shaped his views on thrift, self-reliance, and the value of sound judgment. He worked as a boy at Buffett's grandfather's grocery store. After serving in WWII as a meteorologist, he studied at Harvard Law School (without an undergraduate degree) and practiced law before transitioning to investing. He co-founded a law firm (Munger, Tolles & Olson) and ran his own investment partnership before merging his interests with Buffett's at Berkshire Hathaway. He died in November 2023 at age 99, just weeks before his 100th birthday.

## Key Contributions

- **Architect of Berkshire's investment philosophy** — shifted Buffett from pure Graham-style "cigar butt" value investing toward buying excellent businesses at fair prices; the Berkshire Hathaway of Sees Candies, Coca-Cola, and Apple reflects Munger's influence
- **The Latticework of [[mental-models|Mental Models]]** — Munger's signature intellectual contribution: accumulating mental models from many disciplines to reason more clearly across domains
- **The Psychology of Human Misjudgment** — his landmark talk cataloging 25+ cognitive biases; predates and parallels much of behavioral economics
- **[[inversion|Inversion]]** — popularized the Jacobi maxim "invert, always invert" as a core problem-solving technique
- **Daily Journaling and Reading** — lifelong learner who read voraciously across biology, physics, history, mathematics, and psychology into his 90s

## Key Works
- *Poor Charlie's Almanack* (collected speeches and talks, various editions; 2023 expanded edition published by Stripe Press)
