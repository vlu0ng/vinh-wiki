# Ethan Mollick

Professor at the Wharton School (University of Pennsylvania), researcher in innovation and entrepreneurship, and one of the most prominent academic voices on the practical implications of generative AI.

## Background

Mollick is an Associate Professor at Wharton whose research focuses on innovation, entrepreneurship, and education technology. Unlike most AI commentators, he approached LLMs as an early hands-on user — incorporating ChatGPT into his classes within days of its launch in November 2022 — and has since run numerous research studies on AI's actual effects on knowledge work.

## Key Contributions

- **Co-Intelligence** (2024) — a widely-read book on how to live and work with AI, pitched at general audiences and grounded in both academic research and personal experimentation
- **The [[jagged-frontier|Jagged Frontier]]** — a key concept describing the irregular, invisible boundary of AI capability; coined with co-authors based on empirical studies of AI performance on professional tasks
- Newsletter and public writing that makes AI research accessible to practitioners
- Early classroom research showing AI's productivity impact on business students
