# Anne-Laure Le Cunff

French-Algerian neuroscientist, writer, and founder of Ness Labs; author of *Tiny Experiments*.

## Background

Le Cunff was raised in Paris, the daughter of a French father (from Dinan, Brittany) and an Algerian mother (from Sidi Okba). She was the first woman in her family to pursue higher education. After studying science and business, she joined Google, where she worked in digital health — rising to a global role before resigning at 27 to found a startup. After the startup failed, she returned to school to study neuroscience, eventually completing a Ph.D. She built Ness Labs — a newsletter and community focused on mindful productivity and applied neuroscience — to 100,000+ readers while completing her academic training.

## Key Contributions

- **Ness Labs** — newsletter, community, and small business at the intersection of neuroscience, productivity, and personal growth
- ***Tiny Experiments*** (2024) — a book arguing for an experimental, curiosity-driven approach to life and career over linear goal-setting; grounded in neuroscience of learning
- **The "100 articles in 100 workdays" experiment** — her founding pact for Ness Labs; demonstrated that small, bounded commitments driven by curiosity can compound into substantial impact
- Research on how different brains learn differently using EEG and eye-tracking
