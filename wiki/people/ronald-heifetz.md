# Ronald Heifetz

Professor at Harvard Kennedy School and co-founder of the Center for Public Leadership. Developer of the Adaptive
Leadership framework, one of the most influential theories of leadership in organizational settings.

## Core Framework

Heifetz's foundational insight: most leadership failures occur because people apply *technical* solutions to
*adaptive* problems. Technical problems have known solutions and can be solved by authorities/experts. Adaptive
challenges require people to change their own values, beliefs, and behaviors — and no authority can do that for them.

This distinction — developed in *Leadership Without Easy Answers* (1994) and refined in *Leadership on the Line*
(2002, with Linsky) — reframes leadership as primarily about *mobilizing people to face hard work*, not about having
the right answers.

## Key Books

- *Leadership Without Easy Answers* (1994) — founding statement of the adaptive leadership framework
- *Leadership on the Line* (2002, with Marty Linsky) — the personal risks of leadership; how to stay alive
  politically while doing adaptive work
- *The Practice of Adaptive Leadership* (2009, with Linsky and Grashow) — a field manual with exercises and tools

## Key Contributions

- The technical/adaptive distinction — the most practically useful diagnostic tool in leadership development
- The balcony/dance floor metaphor — leaders must alternate between immersion in the action and observing the system
- Productive disequilibrium — the idea that change requires some heat; the leader's job is to regulate the
  temperature, not eliminate it
- Faction mapping — understanding who gains and who loses from change proposals
