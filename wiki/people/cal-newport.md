# Cal Newport

Computer science professor at Georgetown University and author known for challenging conventional productivity
advice. His recurring thesis: the way knowledge workers are taught to work — always-on, multitasking, inbox-obsessed
— is not just unpleasant but counterproductive.

## Key Works

- *Deep Work* (2016) — argues that the ability to focus without distraction on cognitively demanding tasks is
  increasingly rare and increasingly valuable
- *Digital Minimalism* (2019) — applies similar logic to social media and technology consumption
- *A World Without Email* (2021) — critiques the hyperactive hive mind as an organizational dysfunction
- *Slow Productivity* (2024) — historical argument that great creators worked less frantically than we imagine;
  three-principle framework for a saner approach

## Key Contributions

- Coined "deep work" as a technical term for focused, distraction-free cognitive effort
- Documented the "pseudo-productivity" trap: visible busyness mistaken for valuable output
- Used historical case studies (Jane Austen, Copernicus, Marie Curie, Jewel) to argue that the slow, variable
  pace of master-level work is a feature, not a flaw
- Developed the three principles of [[slow-productivity]]: do fewer things, work at a natural pace, obsess over quality
