# David Stockman

Reagan's budget director (1981–1985), Wall Street veteran, and contrarian critic of both parties' fiscal and monetary policies.

## Background

Born in Michigan, Stockman worked in Republican politics before serving in Congress and then as Ronald Reagan's first director of the Office of Management and Budget. He was the architect of Reagan's initial budget plan — and one of its most clear-eyed critics after leaving the administration. He later spent two decades on Wall Street in leveraged buyout finance, including an ill-fated $4 billion auto parts supplier that went bankrupt.

## Key Contributions

- **The Triumph of Politics** (1986) — his first memoir documenting the failure of Reagan's fiscal revolution; an insider account of how political constraints overwhelmed economic principles
- **The Great Deformation** (2013) — a sweeping critique of American monetary and fiscal policy from FDR through Obama; argues that [[crony-capitalism|crony capitalism]] and [[bubble-finance|bubble finance]] have corrupted American democracy
- Coined the "supply-side" critique from the inside: as budget director he understood that the deficit math didn't work even while publicly defending it
- One of the few figures to criticize both parties' bailout cultures from a consistent free-market, [[sound-money|sound-money]] perspective
