# Jessie Inchauspée

Biochemist and science communicator known online as the "Glucose Goddess." Author of *Glucose Revolution* (2022) and
*The Glucose Goddess Method* (2023). Her work translates continuous glucose monitor (CGM) data and metabolic research
into practical, accessible dietary interventions.

## Background

Holds a degree in mathematics from Sciences Po Paris and a degree in genetics from King's College London. Her personal
interest in glucose monitoring began after a health scare; she began sharing CGM data and experiments on Instagram,
building a following before writing her books.

## Key Contributions

- *Glucose Revolution* (2022) — translated glucose curve science into 10 practical "hacks" for flattening spikes
  without extreme dietary restriction
- Introduced the "glucose curve" as an intuitive visual metaphor for metabolic health — spikes and crashes vs.
  steady cruising altitude
- Emphasized: *flatten your glucose curves → automatically flatten fructose and insulin curves*
- Core message: you don't need to eliminate foods; you need to manage the *sequence*, *timing*, and *pairing* of foods
  to control glucose impact

## Caveats in Her Own Work

Inchauspée explicitly acknowledges: glucose isn't everything. Industrial processed oils, alcohol, and other factors
matter for health but don't register as glucose spikes. She resists prescribing a "diet" and instead offers tactics
that work within any dietary framework.
