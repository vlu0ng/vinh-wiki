# Partners in Health (PIH)

Global nonprofit health organization co-founded by Dr. Paul Farmer and Jim Yong Kim. Operates hospitals and community health worker programs in 11 countries, including Haiti, Rwanda, Sierra Leone, and Peru.

## Mission

Provide high-quality healthcare to the world's poorest populations, with the explicit argument that geography and poverty should not determine who receives treatment.

## Relevance to This Wiki

- Supports **Lakka Government Hospital** in Sierra Leone, where [[john-green]] visited and first encountered active TB cases up close
- PIH pioneered the **community health worker (CHW)** model: training local community members as healthcare extenders who follow patients home, ensure medication adherence, and address social barriers to treatment
- Paul Farmer's work on TB in Haiti demonstrated that drug-resistant TB could be treated in resource-limited settings when dismissed as "too expensive" by global health institutions

## Key Figure: Paul Farmer (1959–2022)

- Co-founder of PIH
- Physician-anthropologist who argued that poverty itself is the primary driver of preventable disease
- His career is documented in Tracy Kidder's *Mountains Beyond Mountains* (2003)

## Relation to TB

PIH helped demonstrate that multi-drug resistant TB (MDR-TB) treatment is both medically and logistically feasible in low-resource settings — a key argument in the narrative that [[tuberculosis-and-global-health]] presents.
